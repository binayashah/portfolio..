My Chorchet Projects

I love working on crochet projects because they allow me to express my creativity while making something beautiful and useful. From traditional wears and flower bouqets to cute amigurumi and home décor, each piece I create is made with care and passion. Crochet has become a relaxing hobby for me, and I enjoy experimenting with new patterns, colors, and stitches. It’s always rewarding to see a project come to life, one loop at a time.
Here are some details about my works.

Bunny Doll

I made a cute crochet bunny doll using soft yarn and simple stitches. It has long ears, a tiny nose, and a little bow that makes it even more adorable. This project helped me practice shaping and stitching, and it’s one of my favorite handmade creations.

Live Demo

Silam Sakma

One of my special projects is making Silam Sakma, a traditional badge worn by the Rai and Limbu communities. I carefully craft each piece with respect for its cultural importance, using fabric, thread, and traditional designs. This project helps me stay connected to my roots while also improving my handmade skills.

Live Demo

Chorchet Tulips

I enjoy making crochet tulips using bright and colorful yarn. They look like real flowers and never fade, making them perfect for home decoration or as handmade gifts. This project helps me practice detail work and brings a lot of joy to those who receive them.

Live Demo

Amigrumi Keyring

I make cute amigurumi keyrings using soft yarn and small crochet hooks. These tiny handmade toys come in fun shapes like animals or fruits and are perfect to hang on bags or keys. They’re quick to make and always bring a smile to people’s faces.

Live Demo

Wall Silam Sakma

I create wall Silam Sakma as a decorative piece inspired by the traditional badge of the Rai and Limbu communities. Made with fabric and thread, it reflects cultural pride and is perfect for displaying at home or during special events. This project helps me blend tradition with creativity. Live Demo

Amigrumi Charmender

I made an amigurumi Charmander using orange yarn and careful stitching to capture its cute features. With its tiny flame tail and big eyes, it looks just like the Pokémon character. This project was fun and challenging, and it's one of my favorite handmade toys.

About my self!

My name is Binaya Shah, and I live in Dharan, Nepal. I have recently completed my 10th-grade exams and am eager to continue learning and growing both academically and creatively. I am passionate about crochet and handmade crafts, which allow me to express my creativity and connect with my cultural heritage.
Through my projects, I create a variety of beautiful and meaningful items such as amigurumi dolls, crochet tulips, keyrings, and traditional Silam Sakma badges. Each piece I make is crafted with care and attention to detail, reflecting my dedication to improving my skills and producing quality work. I enjoy exploring new patterns, experimenting with colors, and challenging myself with different techniques.
My work is not only a creative outlet but also a way to preserve and celebrate the traditions of the Rai and Limbu communities. I take pride in combining modern craft techniques with cultural elements, which adds a unique and personal touch to my projects.
I am excited to continue developing my craft, learning new skills, and sharing my creations with others. Through my portfolio, I hope to showcase my passion, creativity, and commitment to quality handmade work.
